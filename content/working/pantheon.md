abstract: Lists and rankings detailing popularity, impact, and greatness, are ubiquitous across a variety of fields. Beyond domain specific lists there is a long tradition of creating rankings of important, impactful, and popular historical figures (Friedman, 1998; Hart, 1978, 1992; Skiena & Ward, 2013). These rankings are often biased in favor of individuals from Western regions and more distant historical figures. We apply a novel modeling framework, initially developed for cross-era comparisons in baseball, to a comprehensive data set on the impact of historical figures. This modeling framework balances an individual's success against contemporaneous peers relative to the era's population size. Under this framework, highly impactful individuals must stand above their contemporaries, with weight given to those who distinguished themselves within large source populations. Our results elevate the historical standing of individuals from non-Western regions and larger, more recent population pools, challenging traditional biases. This approach to era-neutral historical ranking not only reshapes our understanding of impactful figures but also has broad implications for historiography and quantitative assessments of influence.
